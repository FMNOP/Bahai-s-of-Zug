Core Principles

Inspiring the build of a better world.

The teachings of the Bahá'í Faith inspire individuals and families in Zug to contribute to the spiritual and material progress of society.

🎨

Unity in Diversity

Just as flowers of different colors make a garden beautiful, the diversity of the human family makes our world richer. We believe in overcoming prejudices of all kinds.

💡

Search for Truth

We believe in the harmony of science and religion. Science helps us understand how things work, while religion guides us on how to act with love, kindness, and justice.

🌱

Inherent Nobility

Every human being is born with beautiful qualities inside—like kindness, courage, and truthfulness. Education is the key that unlocks these hidden gems.

"The earth is but one country..."

Bahá'í beliefs address essential themes such as the oneness of God and religion, the oneness of humanity, the progressive unfolding of religious truth, the equality of women and men, the centrality of justice, and the importance of education for children worldwide.

What we do

Engaged in building vibrant local communities.

We work together with neighbors, friends, and families to create welcoming spaces for spiritual growth and community service.

Kids (Ages 5-10)

Children's Classes

Fun and creative classes focused on developing spiritual virtues like kindness, sharing, and honesty. Children learn through stories, cooperative games, songs, and arts.

Classes help children understand their nobility and build moral strength. In the Canton of Zug, kids from all backgrounds join these classes, fostering early habits of mutual respect.

Youth (Ages 11-15)

Junior Youth Groups

A space for adolescents to hang out, discuss meaningful topics (like media, peer pressure, and justice), develop their skills, and design local community service projects.

The Junior Youth Spiritual Empowerment Program supports youth during a crucial stage of growth. They learn to analyze their environment and apply spiritual principles to solve real-world problems.

Adults & Families

Devotional Gatherings

Simple gatherings of friends and neighbors to share prayers, read uplifting passages, and enjoy spiritual music. It's a peaceful space to reflect and bind hearts together.

Devotionals have no formal ritual. Guests are welcome to share prayers from their own faith, scriptures, or simply enjoy the peaceful environment, reinforcing spiritual ties.

Youth & Adults

Study Circles

Small group circles studying spiritual writings and translating themes directly into action. Participants learn how to lead children's classes or organize service events.

Study circles combine study and active service. Using curricula from the Ruhi Institute, they build capacity in participants to serve their local communities effectively.

Our Journey

Bahá'í Faith in Switzerland & Zug

The Bahá'í Faith has been present in Switzerland since the beginning of the 20th century. Click the milestones below to discover our history and local roots in Zug.

1903

First Swiss Bahá'ís

The initial presence of the Faith begins as early Swiss thinkers learn about the teachings of unity and peace.

The teachings of the Bahá'í Faith arrived in Switzerland at the dawn of the 20th century, inspiring local searchers who sought international peace and spiritual renewal. Notable early figures established bridges with local associations in Zurich, Bern, and central Switzerland.

1911

'Abdu'l-Bahá's Swiss Visit

'Abdu'l-Bahá, the son of the Faith's founder, visits Switzerland for a period of rest and informal gatherings.

In September 1911, 'Abdu'l-Bahá visited Geneva and Thonon-les-Bains. This period was primarily dedicated to rest after his exhausting journey from Egypt. Rather than public lectures, he held intimate, informal meetings with early Western Bahá'ís and individual seekers who visited him.

1953

Italo-Swiss Assembly

The election of the joint Italo-Swiss regional assembly coordinates activities across both countries.

In 1953, the first joint regional administrative body, the Italo-Swiss National Spiritual Assembly, was elected. This assembly coordinated activities across both Italy and Switzerland, laying the groundwork for stronger local communities.

1962

Swiss National Assembly

Switzerland elects its independent standalone National Spiritual Assembly, based in Bern.

In 1962, during the global Ten Year Crusade, the independent, sovereign National Spiritual Assembly of the Bahá'ís of Switzerland was officially established. This marked the official administrative autonomy of the Swiss Bahá'í community, with its permanent seat in Bern.

Today

Zug Community Activities

Active local groups hosting activities for all ages, contributing to character education and service in Canton Zug.

Today, the Zug community consists of local Bahá'ís and friends from diverse national origins, actively engaged in building peaceful neighborhoods, hosting moral classes for kids in Cham, Baar, and Zug, and organising community devotionals.
